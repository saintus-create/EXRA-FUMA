The National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) is a premier federal biomedical research agency within the United States Department of Health and Human Services. This institute conducts and funds extensive clinical research and data collection.

## NIMH's Role in ADHD Research

NIMH provides highly vetted pharmacological reference materials, public educational statistics, and clinical trial results regarding neurodevelopmental and mental health conditions for global scientific and public review.

### Research Funding

NIMH funds extensive research on ADHD including:

- Basic neuroscience research on brain function
- Clinical trials of new treatments
- Longitudinal studies on ADHD across the lifespan
- Research on co-occurring conditions
- Studies on genetic and environmental factors

### Clinical Research

The institute conducts research to:

- Understand the causes of ADHD
- Develop better diagnostic tools
- Test new treatment approaches
- Evaluate existing interventions
- Identify risk and protective factors

### Data Collection and Statistics

NIMH collects and disseminates:

- Prevalence data on ADHD
- Treatment outcome statistics
- Clinical trial results
- Research findings and publications
- Evidence-based treatment guidelines

## ADHD Information from NIMH

### What is ADHD?

According to NIMH, Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder is a disorder marked by an ongoing pattern of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity that interferes with functioning or development.

### Signs and Symptoms

NIMH identifies three types of ADHD:

1. **Predominantly Inattentive Presentation** - Difficulty with organization and focus
2. **Predominantly Hyperactive-Impulsive Presentation** - Fidgeting, restlessness, impulsivity
3. **Combined Presentation** - Both inattentive and hyperactive-impulsive symptoms

### Risk Factors

Research has identified several risk factors including:

- Genetics
- Brain injury
- Exposure to environmental risks during pregnancy
- Low birth weight
- Premature birth

### Treatment Options

NIMH provides information on evidence-based treatments:

- Medication
- Psychotherapy
- Behavior therapy
- School accommodations
- Parent training
