This checklist is designed to help teachers create a classroom environment that supports children with ADHD to learn, participate, regulate emotions, and feel successful at school. It reflects contemporary, strengths-based and neuro-affirming practice.

---

# 1. Physical Environment

## Classroom Layout

- Classroom pathways are clear and uncluttered
- Students can access learning materials independently
- Seating minimises unnecessary distractions where possible
- Flexible seating options are available (e.g. wobble cushions, standing desk, floor seating)
- Quiet or low-stimulation spaces are available for regulation
- Visual clutter on walls is purposeful and not overwhelming
- Lighting and noise levels are considered for sensory comfort
- High-traffic or noisy areas are minimised for students who need focus support

## Sensory Supports

- Movement opportunities are built into the day
- Sensory tools are available and acceptable to use
- Students can access water and movement breaks when needed
- Headphones or noise reduction supports are available if appropriate
- Students are not punished for sensory regulation needs

---

# 2. Teaching and Learning Practices

## Instructional Supports

- Instructions are clear, concise and broken into manageable steps
- Important information is presented both verbally and visually
- Visual schedules and routines are consistently used
- Learning intentions and expectations are explicit
- Tasks are scaffolded and chunked into smaller parts
- Extra processing time is provided where needed
- Students are supported to begin tasks, not just complete them
- Teachers check for understanding rather than assuming comprehension

## Assessment and Feedback

- Multiple modes of assessment are available
- Feedback is specific, timely and strengths-based
- Students understand criteria for success
- Self-assessment opportunities are provided
- Progress is celebrated, not just achievement

## Differentiation

- Tasks are adjusted to match individual needs and strengths
- Choice is offered where appropriate
- Extension activities are available for students who need them
- Support is provided without drawing attention to differences

---

# 3. Social and Emotional Support

## Relationship Building

- Teachers take time to know each student individually
- Positive relationships are prioritised
- Students feel seen, heard and valued
- Trust is built through consistency and respect

## Behaviour Support

- Behaviour is understood as communication
- Proactive strategies are used rather than reactive
- Clear expectations are communicated positively
- Natural consequences are used where possible
- Restorative approaches are favoured over punitive ones

## Self-Regulation Support

- Students are taught self-regulation strategies
- Co-regulation is available when needed
- Emotions are validated and normalised
- Students are supported to identify their needs

---

# 4. Communication and Partnership

## With Students

- Student voice is valued and sought
- Students are involved in decision-making about their learning
- Strengths are identified and celebrated
- Goals are set collaboratively

## With Families

- Regular, positive communication is maintained
- Families are seen as partners in support
- Cultural and family backgrounds are respected
- Concerns are shared early and constructively

## With Colleagues

- Collaboration with support staff is prioritised
- Professional development is sought and shared
- Successes and challenges are discussed openly
- Resources and strategies are shared

---

This checklist is a starting point. Every classroom and every student is different. Use this as a guide and adapt it to meet the specific needs of your students and context.
