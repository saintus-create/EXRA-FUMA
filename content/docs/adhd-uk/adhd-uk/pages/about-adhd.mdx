ADHD UK provides comprehensive information about Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) to support individuals, families, and professionals.

## What is ADHD?

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects how people think, process information, and regulate their behavior. It is one of the most common neurodevelopmental disorders, affecting both children and adults.

## Key Characteristics

ADHD is characterized by persistent patterns of:

- **Inattention** - Difficulty sustaining focus, organizing tasks, and following through on activities
- **Hyperactivity** - Excessive movement, restlessness, and difficulty remaining seated
- **Impulsivity** - Acting without thinking, interrupting others, and difficulty waiting

## Types of ADHD

1. **Predominantly Inattentive** - Mainly struggles with attention and organization
2. **Predominantly Hyperactive-Impulsive** - Mainly struggles with hyperactivity and impulsivity
3. **Combined Type** - Experiences both inattentive and hyperactive-impulsive symptoms

## Prevalence

ADHD affects approximately 5% of children and 2.5% of adults worldwide. However, many cases remain undiagnosed, particularly in adults and girls.

## Impact on Daily Life

ADHD can affect various aspects of life including:

- Academic performance and educational attainment
- Work productivity and career progression
- Social relationships and family dynamics
- Mental health and emotional wellbeing
- Daily organization and time management
