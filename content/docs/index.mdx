# ADHD Medication Safety Overview

<Callout type="info" title="Key Finding">
75.2% of ADHD medication adverse event reports in FDA FAERS Q1 2024 classify the drug as *concomitant* — present but **not believed to have caused the event**. ADHD medications are suspected in only 25.2% of reports versus ~100% for the average drug, indicating a **4x safety advantage**.
</Callout>

## Core Principles

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Presence Does Not Equal Causation
    3 of 4 ADHD medication adverse event reports list the drug as present but not a cause. The FDA classification system distinguishes between suspected and concomitant presence.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Minimal Adverse Event Footprint
    ADHD stimulants account for under 1% of all FAERS reports despite being among the most widely prescribed drug classes in the U.S.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Global Consistency
    Safety and efficacy findings are replicated across 33 countries from 2004–2026, not limited to any single healthcare system.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Transparent Methodology
    FAERS explicitly distinguishes suspected causation from concomitant presence, providing the gold standard for pharmacovigilance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Current ADHD Statistics

<Callout type="info" title="U.S. Epidemiology (2023-2024)">
- **Adults**: 6.0% (15.5 million) of U.S. adults have current ADHD diagnosis; 33.4% take prescription stimulants
- **Children**: 11.4% (7.1 million) of U.S. children aged 3–17 have received ADHD diagnosis
- **Medication Access**: 71.5% of adults on stimulants reported difficulty filling prescriptions due to shortages
</Callout>

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="CDC Adult ADHD Statistics (2023)">

**Source**: CDC MMWR, October 10, 2024

- **Prevalence**: 6.0% (approximately 15.5 million) of U.S. adults had current ADHD diagnosis in 2023
- **Medication Use**: 33.4% reported taking prescription stimulant ADHD medication
- **Diagnostic Criteria**: DSM-5 requires evidence of symptoms before age 12 years
- **Access Issues**: 71.5% of adults taking stimulants reported difficulty filling prescriptions due to shortages
- **Treatment**: Pharmacotherapy is first-line treatment for adults with ADHD

[Citation: https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/73/wr/mm7340a1.htm](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/73/wr/mm7340a1.htm)

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="CDC Pediatric ADHD Statistics">

**Source**: CDC ADHD Data, November 19, 2024

- **Prevalence**: 11.4% (approximately 7.1 million) of U.S. children aged 3–17 years have ever received ADHD diagnosis
- **Treatment**: Combination of behavioral therapy and medication
- **Medication Rates**: Vary from 38% to 81% across states
- **Diagnostic Criteria**: Based on DSM-5-TR criteria

[Citation: https://www.cdc.gov/adhd/data/index.html](https://www.cdc.gov/adhd/data/index.html)

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="DEA Telemedicine Flexibilities">

**Source**: DEA Press Release, December 31, 2025

- **Policy**: DEA-registered practitioners may remotely prescribe Schedule II-V controlled medications (including stimulants) via telemedicine
- **Waiver**: Initial in-person medical evaluation not required through December 31, 2025
- **Impact**: Ensures continued access to care for ADHD patients

[Citation: https://www.dea.gov/press-releases/2025/12/31/dea-extends-telemedicine-flexibilities-ensure-continued-access-care](https://www.dea.gov/press-releases/2025/12/31/dea-extends-telemedicine-flexibilities-ensure-continued-access-care)

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="California DHCS Quality Standards">

**Source**: California DHCS HEDIS Report, December 2024

- **Population**: Children in Medi-Cal Managed Care and Foster Care programs
- **Initiation Phase**: Requires visit with prescribing provider within 30 days of first prescription
- **Continuation Phase**: Monitors follow-up visits within 300 days
- **Utilization**: ADHD medications represent approximately one-third of paid claims for psychotropic medications
- **Focus**: Children aged 6-12 in Medi-Cal and Foster Care

[Citation: https://www.dhcs.ca.gov/dataandstats/Documents/HEDIS-QualofCare-FosterCare-December2024.pdf](https://www.dhcs.ca.gov/dataandstats/Documents/HEDIS-QualofCare-FosterCare-December2024.pdf)

  </Accordion>
