# Managed Care's Guide to Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder Medication Safety

<Callout type="info" title="Primary Finding">
75.2% of Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder medication adverse event reports in Food and Drug Administration Adverse Event Reporting System Q1 2024 classify the drug as *concomitant* — present but **not believed to have caused the event**. Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder medications are suspected in only 25.2% of reports vs. ~100% for the average drug — a **4x safety advantage**.
</Callout>

## Four Key Principles

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Presence Does Not Equal Causation
    3 of 4 Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder medication adverse event reports list the drug as a bystander, not a cause. The Food and Drug Administration's own classification system draws a clear line between suspected and concomitant presence.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Minimal Adverse Event Footprint
    Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder stimulants account for under 1% of all Adverse Event Reporting System reports despite being among the most widely prescribed drug classes in the U.S.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Global Consistency
    Safety and efficacy findings replicated across 33 countries from 2004–2026 — not an artifact of any single healthcare system.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Transparent Methodology
    Adverse Event Reporting System explicitly distinguishes suspected causation from concomitant presence — the gold standard for pharmacovigilance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Current Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder Statistics & Regulatory Context

<Callout type="info" title="U.S. Epidemiology (2023-2024)">
- **Adults**: 6.0% (15.5 million) of U.S. adults have current Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder diagnosis; 33.4% take prescription stimulants
- **Children**: 11.4% (7.1 million) of U.S. children aged 3–17 have received Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder diagnosis
- **Medication Access**: 71.5% of adults on stimulants reported difficulty filling prescriptions due to shortages
</Callout>

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Centers for Disease Control and Prevention Adult Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder Statistics (2023)">

**Source**: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention MMWR, October 10, 2024

- **Prevalence**: 6.0% (approximately 15.5 million) of U.S. adults had current Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder diagnosis in 2023
- **Medication Use**: 33.4% reported taking prescription stimulant Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder medication
- **Diagnostic Criteria**: Diagnostic and Statistical Manual-5 requires evidence of symptoms before age 12 years
- **Access Issues**: 71.5% of adults taking stimulants reported difficulty filling prescriptions due to shortages
- **Treatment**: Pharmacotherapy is first-line treatment for adults with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder

[Citation: https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/73/wr/mm7340a1.htm](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/73/wr/mm7340a1.htm)

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Centers for Disease Control and Prevention Pediatric Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder Statistics">

**Source**: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder Data, November 19, 2024

- **Prevalence**: 11.4% (approximately 7.1 million) of U.S. children aged 3–17 years have ever received Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder diagnosis
- **Treatment**: Combination of behavioral therapy and medication
- **Medication Rates**: Vary from 38% to 81% across states
- **Diagnostic Criteria**: Based on Diagnostic and Statistical Manual-5-Text Revision criteria

[Citation: https://www.cdc.gov/adhd/data/index.html](https://www.cdc.gov/adhd/data/index.html)

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Drug Enforcement Administration Telemedicine Flexibilities">

**Source**: Drug Enforcement Administration Press Release, December 31, 2025

- **Policy**: Drug Enforcement Administration-registered practitioners may remotely prescribe Schedule II-V controlled medications (including stimulants) via telemedicine
- **Waiver**: Initial in-person medical evaluation not required through December 31, 2025
- **Impact**: Ensures continued access to care for Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder patients

[Citation: https://www.dea.gov/press-releases/2025/12/31/dea-extends-telemedicine-flexibilities-ensure-continued-access-care](https://www.dea.gov/press-releases/2025/12/31/dea-extends-telemedicine-flexibilities-ensure-continued-access-care)

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="California Department of Health Care Services Quality Standards">

**Source**: California Department of Health Care Services Healthcare Effectiveness Data and Information Set Report, December 2024

- **Population**: Children in Medi-Cal Managed Care and Foster Care programs
- **Initiation Phase**: Requires visit with prescribing provider within 30 days of first prescription
- **Continuation Phase**: Monitors follow-up visits within 300 days
- **Utilization**: Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder medications represent approximately one-third of paid claims for psychotropic medications
- **Focus**: Children aged 6-12 in Medi-Cal and Foster Care

[Citation: https://www.dhcs.ca.gov/dataandstats/Documents/HEDIS-QualofCare-FosterCare-December2024.pdf](https://www.dhcs.ca.gov/dataandstats/Documents/HEDIS-QualofCare-FosterCare-December2024.pdf)

  </Accordion>
