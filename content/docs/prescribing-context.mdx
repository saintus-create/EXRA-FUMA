## LDX Prodrug Mechanism

<Callout type="info" title="Prodrug Design">
Lisdexamfetamine (Vyvanse) is pharmacologically **inactive until converted in the bloodstream** by red blood cell enzymatic hydrolysis into active d-amphetamine.
</Callout>

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Cannot be abused by snorting or injecting">
    Molecular proof that LDX is inactive until blood conversion (2013). The prodrug design ensures enzymatic hydrolysis must occur before any pharmacological effect.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="13-14 hour duration of action">
    Unique blood-based enzymatic conversion provides sustained release over 13-14 hours (2016), eliminating the peaks and troughs of immediate-release formulations.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Consistent across populations">
    Stable pharmacokinetics across ethnic and metabolic groups demonstrated in international studies (2020).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Food and pH independent">
    Gastric pH and food intake do not alter absorption (2012), providing predictable dosing regardless of dietary factors.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Onset within 1.5 hours, duration up to 13 hours">
    Wigal SB, et al. (Clin Ther, 2010) established the clinical onset and duration profile.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>

## HAP Study — Human Abuse Potential Data

<Callout type="info" title="FDA Gold Standard">
The Human Abuse Potential (HAP) study uses experienced stimulant users — required by FDA protocol to establish the theoretical maximum abuse signal. These findings apply to extraordinary conditions, not ordinary therapeutic populations.
</Callout>

## Untreated ADHD Risk

<Callout type="warn" title="Clinical Risk">
The clinical risk of **not treating** ADHD must be weighed against any prescribing concerns. Formulary barriers that delay or deny treatment carry their own documented risk profile.
</Callout>

<Cards>
  <Card title="2x Higher Mortality" description="Untreated ADHD linked to 2x higher mortality from accidents. Dalsgaard S, et al., The Lancet (2015)." />
  <Card title="208 Reports on Mortality" description="Evidence proving untreated ADHD causes higher mortality and long-term morbidity. Clinical Guideline (2021)." />
  <Card title="Reduced Mood Disorders" description="Active treatment reduces incidence of secondary mood disorders. Caye A, et al., Mol Psychiatry (2019)." />
  <Card title="Workplace Impact" description="Treatment mitigates workplace impairment and workforce loss. de Graaf R, et al. (2008)." />
</Cards>

## Step Therapy Evidence

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Step therapy increases total costs">
    Restrictive insurance policies increase total costs when accounting for ER visits, treatment failures, and workforce loss (2015).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Generics are not equivalent">
    Different release curves produce different clinical outcomes. Generic substitution is not clinically interchangeable in all patients (2019).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Adherence advantage for LDX">
    Patients stay on Vyvanse significantly longer than generic salts, reducing treatment discontinuation and relapse (2019).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Individualized dosing is medically necessary">
    Against "cookie-cutter" dosing — clinical evidence justifies medical necessity for dose variation based on individual response (2015).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Switching class is standard protocol">
    If one stimulant class fails, the other must be tried before concluding treatment failure (2007).
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
