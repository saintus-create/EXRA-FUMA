## Policy Context

<Callout type="info">
Step therapy and prior authorization policies applied to ADHD medications are not supported by the FAERS safety profile. **Adderall (6.8% suspected rate)** has a lower causation signal than **Tylenol (11.0%)**, which requires no authorization at any formulary tier.
</Callout>

## Evidence Summary

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Consistent Across All Drugs
    The **75.2% concomitant rate** is consistent across all 7 ADHD drugs tracked in FAERS Q1 2024. This is not a single-drug anomaly — every ADHD medication shows the same pattern of being listed as a bystander rather than a cause.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Minimal FAERS Footprint
    ADHD stimulants account for **0.79%** of all FAERS reports (1,341 of 169,640) despite being among the most prescribed drug classes in the United States.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Lower Than OTC Drugs
    Adderall's **6.8%** suspected rate is lower than Tylenol (11.0%), Aspirin (13.3%), and Advil (13.8%) — all unrestricted over-the-counter medications.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### FDA's Own Classifications
    These figures are the FDA's own adverse event role classifications — not industry-sponsored interpretations or cherry-picked statistics.
  </Step>
</Steps>
