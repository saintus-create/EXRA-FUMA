## Product Roles

The FDA Adverse Event Reporting System (FAERS) classifies each drug in an adverse event report into one of three roles. Understanding these roles is essential for interpreting safety data correctly.

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Suspected">
    The drug is believed to have caused or contributed to the adverse event. When a drug is listed as **suspected**, the reporter — typically a healthcare professional, patient, or manufacturer — has identified it as the likely cause of harm. A high suspected rate indicates a stronger causation signal in the pharmacovigilance record.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Concomitant">
    The drug was present in the patient's regimen but is **not** believed to have caused the event. A high concomitant rate is a **positive safety signal**. It means the drug appears in adverse event reports because patients take multiple medications simultaneously — not because it caused harm. For ADHD medications, the concomitant rate is **75.2%**, meaning 3 out of 4 reports list the ADHD drug as a bystander.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Interacting">
    Two or more drugs interacted to produce the adverse event. This classification is used when the combination of drugs — rather than any single agent — is believed to be responsible.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>

## Why This Distinction Matters

<Callout type="info">
The average drug across all 18,048 tracked in FAERS Q1 2024 is classified as **suspected** in virtually every report it appears in (~100%). ADHD medications are suspected in only **25.2%** of their reports — a **4x safety advantage**.
</Callout>

| Category | Reports | Suspected Rate | Concomitant Rate |
|----------|---------|----------------|------------------|
| **ADHD Medications** | **1,341** | **25.2%** | **75.2%** |
| All Other Drugs | 168,299 | ~100% | ~0% |

This means that when an ADHD medication appears in an adverse event report, it is overwhelmingly listed as a bystander — present in the patient's medication list but not believed to have caused the reported event.

*Source: FDA FAERS Q1 2024.*
