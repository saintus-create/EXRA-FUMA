## Comparators

<Callout type="warn">
Adderall's 6.8% suspected rate is lower than Tylenol (11.0%), Aspirin (13.3%), and Advil (13.8%) — all of which are available over the counter without any authorization requirement.
</Callout>

The table below compares the FDA FAERS suspected causation rate for ADHD stimulants against common OTC and formulary drugs. A lower suspected rate means the drug is less frequently identified as the cause of adverse events.

| Drug | Suspected Rate | Category |
|------|---------------|----------|
| **Adderall** | **6.8%** | ADHD Stimulant |
| Tylenol (acetaminophen) | 11.0% | OTC — unrestricted |
| Aspirin | 13.3% | OTC — unrestricted |
| Advil (ibuprofen) | 13.8% | OTC — unrestricted |
| **Ritalin** | **14.7%** | ADHD Stimulant |
| Atorvastatin (Lipitor) | 19.7% | Formulary Rx |
| Amlodipine | 20.5% | Formulary Rx |
| Metformin | 24.5% | Formulary Rx |
