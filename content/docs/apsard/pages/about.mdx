The American Professional Society of ADHD and Related Disorders (APSARD) is a United States professional society that brings together a multidisciplinary network of regional and national researchers, clinicians, and healthcare executives.

## Our Mission

The society focuses strictly on the execution of clinical research dissemination, the advancement of professional development standards, and the hosting of scientific conferences to bridge the gap between psychiatric research and practical treatment.

## What We Do

### Clinical Research Dissemination

APSARD works to:

- Disseminate the latest ADHD research findings
- Translate research into clinical practice
- Share evidence-based treatment approaches
- Promote understanding of ADHD and related disorders
- Bridge the research-to-practice gap

### Professional Development Standards

We advance professional standards by:

- Establishing best practices in ADHD care
- Developing guidelines for assessment and treatment
- Promoting excellence in clinical practice
- Supporting professional education and training
- Fostering professional growth and development

### Scientific Conferences

APSARD hosts scientific conferences that:

- Present cutting-edge ADHD research
- Facilitate networking among professionals
- Provide continuing education opportunities
- Showcase innovations in treatment
- Encourage collaboration across disciplines

## Who We Are

Our multidisciplinary network includes:

- Researchers in ADHD and related disorders
- Clinicians including psychiatrists and psychologists
- Healthcare executives and administrators
- Academic researchers and professors
- Mental health professionals
- Neuroscientists and neurologists

## Our Focus Areas

### ADHD and Related Disorders

APSARD addresses not only ADHD but also related conditions including:

- Co-occurring mental health disorders
- Learning disabilities
- Executive function disorders
- Neurodevelopmental conditions
- Associated behavioral and emotional challenges

### Bridging Research and Practice

We work to connect:

- Academic research with clinical application
- Scientific discovery with patient care
- Theoretical knowledge with practical treatment
- Regional innovations with national standards
- Emerging findings with established practices

## Why APSARD Matters

The gap between research and practice can lead to:

- Delayed implementation of effective treatments
- Inconsistent care across regions
- Missed opportunities for improvement
- Outdated practices persisting
- Patients not benefiting from latest advances

APSARD bridges this gap to ensure patients receive the most current, evidence-based care.

## Get Involved

Professionals can join APSARD to:

- Attend our scientific conferences
- Access research dissemination resources
- Contribute to professional standards development
- Network with ADHD professionals
- Stay current with the latest research
