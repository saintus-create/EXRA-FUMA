The Attention Deficit Disorder Association (ADDA) is a virtual organization based in the United States that dedicates its structural focus exclusively to adults with attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder.

## Our Mission

ADDA fills a distinct niche by providing virtual support groups, adult-centric educational webinars, and workplace advocacy initiatives designed to improve professional and personal outcomes for the adult demographic.

## What We Offer

### Virtual Support Groups

ADDA provides online support groups where adults with ADHD can connect, share experiences, and find community. These virtual meetings offer:

- Peer support from others who understand adult ADHD
- Safe space to discuss challenges and successes
- Opportunities to learn coping strategies
- Connection to a supportive community

### Educational Webinars

Our adult-centric educational webinars cover topics relevant to adults with ADHD:

- Workplace strategies and accommodations
- Time management and organization
- Relationship and communication skills
- Mental health and wellness
- Treatment options and medication management

### Workplace Advocacy

ADHD can present unique challenges in the workplace. ADDA provides:

- Information about workplace rights and accommodations
- Guidance on disclosing ADHD to employers
- Strategies for success in professional environments
- Resources for ADHD-friendly workplace practices

## Why Focus on Adults?

While ADHD is often diagnosed in childhood, many adults continue to experience symptoms that significantly impact their lives. ADDA recognizes that:

- Adult ADHD presents unique challenges different from childhood ADHD
- Adults face workplace, relationship, and financial responsibilities
- Late-diagnosed adults need specific support and resources
- The adult ADHD community deserves dedicated advocacy
