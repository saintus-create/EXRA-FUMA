Educators play a crucial role in the success of students with ADHD. CHADD provides resources to help teachers create supportive learning environments.

## Understanding ADHD in the Classroom

ADHD affects approximately 5-11% of school-age children. Students with ADHD may struggle with:

- Sustaining attention during lessons
- Following multi-step instructions
- Organizing materials and completing assignments
- Remaining seated during class
- Waiting their turn
- Controlling impulsive behaviors

## Classroom Strategies

### Environmental Modifications

- Seat the student away from distractions
- Provide a quiet space for test-taking
- Use visual schedules and reminders
- Keep the classroom organized and predictable
- Minimize visual clutter

### Instructional Strategies

- Break instructions into small steps
- Provide both verbal and written directions
- Use multisensory teaching methods
- Incorporate movement into lessons
- Check for understanding frequently

### Assessment Accommodations

- Extended time on tests
- Quiet testing environment
- Breaks during long assessments
- Oral testing options
- Reduced distractions during testing

## Building Positive Relationships

### Communication with Students

- Focus on strengths, not just challenges
- Provide immediate and specific feedback
- Use positive reinforcement
- Be patient and understanding
- Build trust through consistency

### Working with Parents

- Maintain regular communication
- Share successes, not just concerns
- Collaborate on strategies
- Respect family decisions about treatment
- Be a partner in the child's success

## Legal Considerations

Students with ADHD may be eligible for:

- **IEP (Individualized Education Program)** - Under IDEA
- **504 Plan** - Under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act
- **Classroom Accommodations** - To ensure equal access to education
