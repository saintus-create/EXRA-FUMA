Parenting a child with ADHD comes with unique challenges and rewards. CHADD provides the resources and support you need to help your child thrive.

## Understanding Your Child's ADHD

Every child with ADHD is different. Understanding your child's specific challenges and strengths is the first step toward effective support.

## Working with Schools

### Educational Rights

Children with ADHD may be eligible for special education services and accommodations under:

- **IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)** - Provides special education services
- **Section 504** - Ensures equal access to education
- **ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)** - Protects against discrimination

### Advocating for Your Child

- Communicate regularly with teachers
- Request an evaluation if needed
- Participate in IEP or 504 plan meetings
- Monitor your child's progress
- Build positive relationships with school staff

## Managing ADHD at Home

### Structure and Routine

- Establish consistent daily schedules
- Create clear rules and expectations
- Use visual schedules and checklists
- Provide advance notice of changes
- Build in transition time between activities

### Positive Parenting Strategies

- Use positive reinforcement for desired behaviors
- Provide immediate feedback
- Break instructions into small steps
- Use visual aids and reminders
- Focus on strengths, not just challenges

## Treatment Options

### Multimodal Treatment Approach

Effective ADHD treatment often includes:

- **Medication** - When appropriate and prescribed by a physician
- **Behavioral Therapy** - Teaching skills and strategies
- **Parent Training** - Learning effective parenting techniques
- **School Support** - Accommodations and interventions
- **Lifestyle Changes** - Diet, exercise, and sleep

## Finding Support

- Join a CHADD parent support group
- Connect with other parents of children with ADHD
- Work with an ADHD coach
- Stay informed about ADHD research and treatments
- Take care of your own mental health

For more resources, visit [CHADD's Parent Resources](https://chadd.org/for-parents/).
