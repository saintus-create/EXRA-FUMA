The Centre for ADHD Awareness, Canada (CADDAC) is a national, non-profit organization that focuses its operations within Canada to deliver public awareness, policy advocacy, and socio-educational programs.

## Our Mission

The center focuses heavily on organizing national awareness campaigns, offering direct support resources for families, and hosting technical workshops for educators navigating learning environments.

## What We Do

### National Awareness Campaigns

CADDAC organizes awareness campaigns to:

- Increase public understanding of ADHD across Canada
- Reduce stigma and misconceptions about ADHD
- Promote early identification and intervention
- Share accurate information about ADHD
- Build support for the ADHD community

### Policy Advocacy

We advocate for:

- Improved access to ADHD diagnosis and treatment
- Better educational support for students with ADHD
- Workplace accommodations for adults with ADHD
- Insurance coverage for ADHD services
- Government recognition of ADHD needs

### Socio-Educational Programs

Our programs include:

- Educational workshops for families
- Support groups for parents and individuals
- Training for educators and school staff
- Resources for healthcare providers
- Community education initiatives

### Direct Support Resources

CADDAC provides:

- Information and resources for families
- Guidance on navigating the healthcare system
- Support for educational planning
- Workplace accommodation information
- Mental health and wellness resources

### Technical Workshops for Educators

We offer workshops that help educators:

- Understand ADHD in the classroom
- Implement effective teaching strategies
- Create supportive learning environments
- Navigate individual education plans (IEPs)
- Support students with ADHD effectively

## Why CADDAC Matters

In Canada, individuals with ADHD face challenges including:

- Long wait times for diagnosis
- Inconsistent access to treatment
- Varying levels of school support
- Limited workplace accommodations
- Gaps in public understanding

CADDAC works to address these challenges through advocacy and education.

## Our Impact

Through our work, we aim to:

- Improve understanding of ADHD across Canada
- Reduce barriers to diagnosis and treatment
- Enhance educational support for students
- Promote workplace inclusion for adults
- Build a stronger ADHD community
