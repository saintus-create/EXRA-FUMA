## Purpose

This resource consolidates findings from FDA FAERS data, peer-reviewed literature, and clinical guidelines to support evidence-based evaluations of ADHD medication safety.

## Scope

- FDA FAERS Data: Analysis of 169,640 reports across 18,048 agents including 1,341 ADHD-specific events
- Global Research: Synthesis of 300+ peer-reviewed studies spanning 33 nations from 2004 through 2026
- Clinical Trials: Examination of 115+ domestic trials including pharmacokinetic studies and meta-analyses
- Abuse Potential Data: Standardized Human Abuse Potential results using cohorts of experienced stimulant users
- Utilization Management: Empirical data on clinical outcomes and fiscal consequences of step therapy

## Regulatory Alignment

Data and analyses comply with federal standards for benefits and beneficiary protections. This guide ensures safety-related utilization management does not impede access to basic benefits or violate anti-discrimination mandates.

## Key Definitions

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Suspected">
    Identifies a pharmaceutical agent believed by the reporter to have contributed directly to a specific adverse medical event.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Concomitant">
    Signifies that the medication was present within the patient system at the time of the event although the reporter does not attribute the cause of the harm to the drug.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Concomitant Rate">
    Represents the percentage of total reports where the drug is classified as present but non-causal. Higher rates suggest the medication is less frequently identified as a source of harm relative to its frequency of use.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Medical Necessity">
    Serves as the primary threshold for coverage. All covered items and services must be reasonable and necessary for the diagnosis or treatment of illness or injury.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>

## LDX Pharmacokinetic Context

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Prodrug Activation
    Lisdexamfetamine is an inactive precursor formed by an amino acid and a stimulant. The speed of activation dictates systemic exposure.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Rapid Metabolizer Profile
    In patients with high metabolic velocity, the breakdown of the precursor occurs efficiently. The body purges the resulting active stimulant with speed that may halve the effective duration — resulting in 3–6 hours of functional coverage.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Supratherapeutic Rationale
    For patients with larger frames or higher metabolic velocity, the amount of agent per unit of mass is the primary metric. The inattentive subtype requires higher concentrations to stabilize executive functions.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Divided Administration
    Providing a second dose at mid-day reloads the body with the inactive molecule and initiates a second cycle of transformation, extending focus without requiring a dangerously high single peak.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Objective Surveillance
    Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM) provides objective evidence of rapid clearance. Measuring blood concentration ensures peak levels (Cmax) do not reach toxicity.
  </Step>
</Steps>
