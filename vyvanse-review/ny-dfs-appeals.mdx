# NY DFS External Appeal Decisions: Overturned Denials for Vyvanse Coverage

## Overview

All five cases share a common outcome: the insurance carrier's denial of coverage for Vyvanse (lisdexamfetamine) was overturned. The appeals were primarily based on medical necessity or a formulary exception.

## Summary of Key Findings

| Case Number | Diagnosis | Treatment | Health Plan Decision | Key Rationale for Overturn |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| 202008-130488 (Vyvanse 80mg/day) | ADHD, Bulimia (Binge Eating) | Vyvanse 40 mg × 2 tabs | Overturned | Patient on effective dose (80mg daily) for over 4 years; clinically appropriate; consistent with prevailing medical standards and FDA indication. |
| 201912-123987 (Vyvanse 40/50/60/70mg) | ADHD Combined Type | Vyvanse 50 mg Capsule | Overturned | Patient tried and failed other medications, had best response to current off-label dose (40mg + 50mg daily) for years; superior efficacy of Vyvanse to some alternatives cited. |
| 202101-134574 (Vyvanse 30mg) | ADHD, Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) | Vyvanse 30mg | Overturned | Patient had efficacy with Vyvanse (FDA approved for ADHD) which has a unique prodrug mechanism; continuing it is in the patient's best interest, even without "try and fail" of all formulary alternatives. |
| 202301-158521 (Vyvanse) | ADHD-Inattentive presentation, Unspecified Anxiety Disorder | Vyvanse (lisdexamfetamine) | Overturned | Patient had reportedly tried/failed two main types of psychostimulants (Methylphenidate and Adderall products); Vyvanse, being a different class/prodrug, was deemed medically necessary. |
| 201911-123101 (Vyvanse) | ADHD, Learning Disability | Vyvanse | Overturned | Patient had long-standing success on Vyvanse; a change would be clinically inappropriate given positive response for years without significant side effects, despite potential lack of documentation for all failed generic trials. |

## The Central Theme of the Appeals

The main conflict in these cases centers on the insurer's requirement for the patient to first "try and fail" formulary alternatives (like generic Adderall, Methylphenidate products, or Atomoxetine) before approving coverage for the non-formulary, brand-name drug Vyvanse.

In every instance, the external review agents determined that Vyvanse was medically necessary and overturned the denial, often citing:

### Established Efficacy
The patient had demonstrated a clear, positive, and long-standing therapeutic response to Vyvanse.

### Unique Mechanism of Action
Vyvanse (lisdexamfetamine) is an inactive prodrug that is activated after ingestion, which is cited as having a lower abuse potential and a different mechanism than other common psychostimulants, making it a distinct treatment option.

### Failed Alternatives
In most cases, the patient had already tried and failed at least some of the alternative psychostimulants.

## Conclusion

The decision-makers concluded that the health plans did not act reasonably or with sound medical judgment in denying coverage for a medication that was providing a clear and effective benefit to the patient.
