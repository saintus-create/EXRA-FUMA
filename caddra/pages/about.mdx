The Canadian ADHD Resource Alliance (CADDRA) is an independent, non-profit, member-directed organization comprising healthcare professionals, clinicians, and researchers.

## Our Mission

CADDRA's central contribution is the development and periodic publication of the Canadian ADHD Practice Guidelines, which serve as a benchmark for the pharmacological and clinical assessment and treatment of ADHD across Canada.

## Canadian ADHD Practice Guidelines

The Canadian ADHD Practice Guidelines are comprehensive, evidence-based guidelines that provide:

- **Assessment Protocols** - Standardized approaches to ADHD diagnosis
- **Treatment Recommendations** - Evidence-based treatment options
- **Pharmacological Guidance** - Medication management strategies
- **Clinical Best Practices** - Standards of care for ADHD
- **Age-Specific Considerations** - Guidelines for children, adolescents, and adults

## Who We Are

CADDRA members include:

- Physicians and psychiatrists
- Psychologists and mental health professionals
- Researchers and academics
- Healthcare providers specializing in ADHD
- Clinical experts in neurodevelopmental disorders

## Our Work

### Research and Evidence

CADDRA supports and conducts research to:

- Advance understanding of ADHD
- Evaluate treatment effectiveness
- Identify best practices in ADHD care
- Contribute to the global ADHD knowledge base

### Professional Education

We provide education and training for healthcare professionals:

- Conference presentations and workshops
- Training programs on ADHD assessment and treatment
- Continuing medical education opportunities
- Resources for clinical practice

### Policy and Advocacy

CADDRA advocates for:

- Improved access to ADHD diagnosis and treatment
- Insurance coverage for ADHD services
- Better support for individuals with ADHD
- Evidence-based healthcare policies

## Why CADDRA Matters

As a member-directed organization, CADDRA ensures that:

- Guidelines are developed by clinical experts
- Recommendations are based on the latest research
- Canadian healthcare context is considered
- Standards reflect best practices in ADHD care

## Accessing Guidelines

The Canadian ADHD Practice Guidelines are available to healthcare professionals and provide the foundation for evidence-based ADHD assessment and treatment across Canada.
